## Jollof Rice

A smoky, flavourful one-pot rice dish and a staple at every Nigerian celebration.

**Serves:** 4–6 | **Prep:** 20 mins | **Cook:** 45 mins

### Ingredients

- 3 cups long-grain parboiled rice
- 6 medium tomatoes, blended
- 3 red bell peppers, blended
- 2 scotch bonnet peppers (atarodo), blended
- 1 large onion (half blended, half sliced)
- ¼ cup vegetable oil
- 2 cups chicken stock
- 2 tsp curry powder
- 1 tsp thyme
- 2 bay leaves
- Salt to taste

### Instructions

1. **Make the tomato base** — Blend tomatoes, red peppers, scotch bonnet, and half the onion into a smooth purée.
2. **Fry the base** — Heat oil in a large pot over medium-high heat. Sauté the sliced onion until soft, then pour in the blended purée. Cook uncovered for 20–25 minutes, stirring frequently, until the paste darkens and the oil floats to the top.
3. **Season** — Stir in curry powder, thyme, bay leaves, and salt. Cook for another 2 minutes.
4. **Add stock & rice** — Pour in the chicken stock. Taste and adjust seasoning. Add the washed rice and stir to combine.
5. **Steam** — Reduce heat to low. Cover the pot tightly (use foil under the lid for a better seal) and cook for 25–30 minutes until the rice is tender and slightly smoky at the bottom.
6. **Serve** — Fluff with a fork and serve with fried chicken, moi moi, or coleslaw.

> **Tip:** The slightly burnt bottom layer (_party jollof_ crust) is a feature, not a bug — it's where the flavour lives.
