# Egusi Soup

A rich and hearty Nigerian soup made with ground melon seeds, vegetables, and assorted meats. A staple in many Nigerian homes and best enjoyed with pounded yam, eba, or fufu.

## Info

- **Prep Time:** 25 minutes
- **Cook Time:** 40 minutes
- **Total Time:** 65 minutes
- **Servings:** 4–6
- **Difficulty:** Medium

## Ingredients:

### Protein
- 500g assorted meat (beef, goat meat, or tripe)
- 1 smoked fish or dried fish (optional)
- 1 cup stockfish (optional)

### Soup Base
- 2 cups ground egusi (melon seeds)
- 1 cup palm oil
- 1 medium onion, chopped
- 3 cups meat stock

### Seasoning
- 2 seasoning cubes
- 1 cup ground crayfish
- 2 scotch bonnet peppers (atarodo), blended
- 1 tsp ground pepper
- Salt to taste

### Vegetables
- 2 cups spinach, pumpkin leaves (ugu), or bitter leaf (washed and chopped)

## Instructions

1. **Cook the meat:**  
   Season the assorted meat with salt, chopped onions, and seasoning cubes. Boil until tender. Reserve the meat stock for later.

2. **Prepare the egusi mixture:**  
   Mix the ground egusi with a small amount of water to form a thick paste.

3. **Heat the palm oil:**  
   In a large pot, heat palm oil over medium heat. Add chopped onions and sauté for 2–3 minutes until fragrant.

4. **Fry the egusi:**  
   Scoop small portions of the egusi paste into the hot oil. Allow it to fry for about 5 minutes without stirring so it forms small lumps.

5. **Add stock and seasonings:**  
   Pour in the reserved meat stock. Add ground crayfish, blended pepper, seasoning cubes, and salt. Stir gently and allow to simmer for 10–15 minutes.

6. **Add proteins:**  
   Add the cooked meat, smoked fish, and stockfish (if using). Stir and simmer for another 5–7 minutes.

7. **Add vegetables:**  
   Stir in the spinach or bitter leaf and cook for 3–5 minutes until the vegetables are tender.

8. **Simmer and serve:**  
   Allow the soup to thicken slightly, adjust seasoning if needed, and serve hot.

## Tips

- For a thicker soup, reduce the amount of stock used.
- Allowing the egusi to fry before stirring helps create the traditional textured lumps.
- Adding smoked fish gives the soup a deeper, richer flavour.
- Bitter leaf should be well washed to remove excessive bitterness.

## Variations

- **Vegetarian Egusi:** Replace meat with mushrooms and vegetable stock.
- **Egusi with Ugu:** Use pumpkin leaves (ugu) instead of spinach for a more traditional flavour.
- **Egusi with Ogbono:** Add 1–2 tablespoons of ogbono powder for a slightly draw (viscous) texture.

Enjoy your homemade Egusi Soup!
